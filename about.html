About

 

UTutors is a tutoring program that matches high-achieving University of Toronto students with Grade 6 or 9 students from the Toronto District School Board.

The COVID-19 outbreak has disrupted learning and made everyday life more challenging, especially for students. Students in elementary and secondary schools had not been attending physical classrooms, nor had they been close to their friends. We can improve the well-being of ourselves and others by building connections and helping one another.

We are a sub-department of UofT aiming to leverage the talent of our students. Our tutors will deliver quality educational mentorship and can feel proud of their efforts in return.

A convincing body of research suggests that tutoring can be an effective means to help individuals, not just for improving academic outcomes but also for mental health. UTutors aims to make it easy and rewarding for UofT students to volunteer their time each week by offering tutoring services to Grade 6 or 9 students.

Tutors first receive orientation training and meet with a UTutors leader before meeting with their matched tutee. After signing up and completing orientation, tutors are connected with their tutee’s parents or guardians to arrange a regular meeting time (two 45-minute sessions per week). Tutors communicate with their tutee and their tutee’s teacher about the topics to be covered. Tutors also serve as role models and can offer general academic advice.

Feedback provided from tutors will allow the program to evolve using best practices.
